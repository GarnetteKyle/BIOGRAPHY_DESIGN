A life in Progress


Childhood Years

1Hi, my name is Garnette Kyle Valenzuela, I was born and raised in Tondo, Manila, and I can’t wait to leave this place. 2My friends always tease me because of my name. 3Some say I was a basketball player because my name is similar to Kevin Garnett. 4And some say my name is derived from Garnet Stone. 5But my name is just literally a logical mix of my parents’ names. 6When I was a kid, I always put myself in trouble. 7There was an accident where I was running and tripped, and my left forearm got sliced by an iron sheet. 8Another time, I was playing with my classmates and tripped again, and my tooth broke. 9I also remember the day my friend almost hit my eye with a pencil. 10I can say that I had a happy childhood. 11But of course, there are always two sides of the coin. 12I also experienced bullying because I was overweight back then. 13I have friends, but there are very few classmates who, for some reason, love bullying. 14I always got involved in fights because of it. 15But I had already built the character of a nice, kind, quiet, and gentle boy in my teachers’ and classmates’ minds, that’s why I was never scolded by our teachers or had a bad record in the Guidance Office. 16I hope they’re doing fine. 17We were kids back then, we make mistakes and still have a lot of lessons to learn. 18Looking back, those early scrapes taught me resilience. 19I learned that pain is temporary but memories last. 20This foundation shaped who I am today.


Teenage Years

1I studied at Jose Abad Santos High School in Binondo, Manila. 2When I was in 7th grade, I had a lot of friends. 3We always played mobile games every vacant time, walking back home together after class. 4But in 8th grade, I felt like I didn’t belong. 5I only had two friends, while the whole class was always together and getting along with each other. 6That’s why at that time, all I did was play online games. 7The school year was still ongoing when COVID came. 8Almost my whole junior high school was conducted online. 9I didn’t know what to feel at that time, whether to be happy to avoid socializing with people or sad because I might have missed one of the happiest stages of my life. 10After 10th grade, everything started to go back to normal. 11Since I spent the whole time playing online games, I took ICT–Computer Programming as my senior high school strand and track. 12I told myself that I cannot live isolating myself; that’s why I started socializing and making friends with everyone, and it went well. 13I met a lot of friends. 14I really loved my senior high school days as they were one of the happiest moments of my life. 15But it is also one of the stages of my life that I want to forget. 16Things turned bitter. 17After graduation, I cut ties. 18I just hope that in college, I will build something again and move forward in life. 19I want to leave those bitter memories behind. 20I am ready for a new chapter.


College Years

1College came, and I still can’t believe that I will be studying at the University of the East under the BSCS program. 2I was considering studying at cheap private colleges, but my aunts insisted that I study here instead. 3I did try to apply to state universities, but I was only able to take the entrance exam at PLM. 4I still remember putting a sad emoticon on the back of my scratch paper because I knew deep inside that I had flunked the exam. 5I don’t even know why I felt sad, knowing that I hadn’t even spent a single day reviewing. 6Then I received an email from PLM, and no, it was not a plot twist showing that I passed. 7It was just to show me that I really failed the exam. 8Now, let's focus on the actual college experience. 9On my first day of class, I entered the room knowing I was early, but for some reason, there was a professor sitting at the front. 10I asked if I was in the correct section, and I was. 11Then, our professor realized that we were not supposed to meet that day, it was supposed to be the next day. 12It was so funny that it happened during the first day that I remember it up to this day. 13Moving forward, I met a lot of friends, especially during my NSTP-ROTC days. 14I experienced many new things I had never tried before, traveling to far places, eating outside, and just enjoying life. 15College is tiring, both physically and mentally. 16To be honest, I sometimes feel like I’m getting left behind, doubting if I really belong in this program. 17But I know deep inside that it is part of the process, and I won’t let it consume me or bring me down. 18Yes, college is tiring, but that is also where you feel alive. 19I still make mistakes, and sometimes I get frustrated, but I try to laugh at myself. 20I always think of the life I want, so I will try my best and keep moving forward.
